About

Prasanna Grace Augustine is a doctoral researcher specialising in children’s literature, trauma recovery, and bibliotherapy. Her research analyses how narrative experiences support emotional regulation, facilitate the comprehension of abstract ideas, and cultivate logical and ethical reasoning in young readers. She further investigates how stories function as cognitive tools that enhance problem‑solving, creativity, and critical thought. Her work positions literature as a vital developmental resource that enables children to process adversity, rebuild disrupted schemas, and engage meaningfully with the world around them.

She is particularly interested in how contemporary texts redefine canonical narratives, diversify character representation, and create spaces for healing, agency, and affective literacy—dimensions that work in tandem with cognitive development in the classroom.
